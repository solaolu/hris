Appraisal Ratings:

RATING

 DESCRIPTION OF PERFORMANCE LEVEL AGAINST EXPECTATIONS

A+ = Outstanding
(100 &above)

Outstanding performance. The jobholder has met and exceed the agreed goals. His/her contribution to the Organization’s results is outstanding and commendable.

A = Excellent
(85 - 99)

Excellent performance. The jobholder has met agreed goals. His/her contribution to the Organization’s results is well above the level of good, well-motivated and able jobholders.

B = Good
(70 - 84)

Successfully achieves key requirements of position. The jobholder has met the agreed goals. His/her contribution to the Organization’s result is above the level of good, well-motivated and able jobholders.

C = Average
(65 - 69)

Achieves requirements of position. The jobholder has achieved some of the agreed goals. His/her contribution to the Organization’s results is well up to expectations. However, there is room for improvement.

D = Needs Improvement
(55 - 64)

Performance has met some but not all standards. The jobholder has not fully met the agreed goals. His/her contribution to the Organization’s result is below the level of good, well-motivated and able jobholders. If performance remains the same at the next review, a Performance Improvement Process should be initiated.

E = Not Acceptable
(0 - 54)

Not meeting performance requirements of position, the jobholder has not met the agreed goals. To contribute to the Organization’s results, urgent improvements are needed. A performance Improvement Process should be initiated immediately

GUIDELINES FOR DISCUSSION AND KEY OUTPUTS OF PERFORMANCE ASSESSMENT WITH THE CANDIDATE

Main points of the discussion

  • General performance evaluation.
  • Performance review of specific work objectives agree on at the last appraisal.
  • Areas of strength/weaknesses (to be developed through T&D and new work assignments).
  • Other next steps (to be kept in focus during next 3 months).
